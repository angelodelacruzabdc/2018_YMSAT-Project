I N N O V A T I O N S
Filipinos have made countless of innovations that brought significant changes to our society.

An antibiotic called Erythromycin is used to treat bacterial infections. It is often given to patients who are allergic to penicillin. It was discovered by Abelardo Aguilar.
Nine specific breeds of rice, which prevented hunger and famine due to its adaptability, were invented by Dr. Rodolfo Aquino.

Fe del Mundo invented the medical incubator which has saved countless of premature infants.

Diosdado Banatao invented different types of computer microchips.

Antibiotics are slowly losing their edge because as time passes by, bacteria are becoming more resistant to them due to its wide use. As of now, many people, including Filipinos, are suffering, some even dying due to antibiotic-resistant bacteria and superbugs.

 Due to the growing resistance to antibiotics, researchers are once again looking into implementing and improving the use of bacteriophages as they were the first antibiotics, used during the early 1930s.
I N N O V A T I O N S
During a convention of the Philippine Society of Microbiology in Manila (July 16-17 2015), David Trudil, advisor to the George Eliava Institute of Bacteriophages in Tbilisi, Georgia, told scientists within the convention that it is time to bring bacteriophage therapy back. As of now, Filipino scientists and researchers are on efforts of bringing bacteriophage therapy to the Philippines.
I N N O V A T I O N S
Only a limited amount of land is allotted for farming and agriculture is given to Filipino farmers due to land conservation and industrial purposes. That is why we are having a struggle on our agricultural needs.

Because of this, the Phil govt. has launched projects about farm mechanization just like the JICA-supported projection of mechanizing farming equipments and showing its difference to farmers. Given the small amount of land used for farming, mechanizing was one of the best solutions in order to maximize the crop yield.
I N N O V A T I O N S
Just recently, the Philippine Genome Center was opened in UP Diliman.

The Philippine Genome Center promises to provide bioinformatics services, DNA sequencing, cutting-edge health and environmental research for the country. It aims to be a center of excellence in gene discovery and genomics research. It combines mathematics, science, technology, and statistics to study living things at a molecular level.
I N N O V A T I O N S
Through rigorous research, The Philippine Genome Center hopes to produce applications in the fields of health and medicine, agriculture, forensics and ethnicity, industry, and the environment that will benefit the country.
